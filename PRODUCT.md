# Product

## Register

product

## Users
A medical student (and peers) studying pharmacology — membrane drug targets
(ion channels, GPCRs, receptors) for the integrated Pharmacology course (Generale, Speciale
Speciale). Context: exam preparation at a desk, mostly on macOS/Safari, switching between deep study
(reading a target's mechanism, rotating a 3D model) and fast review (scanning drug tables and
classifications, jumping to FarmacoFlash flashcards). Bilingual: Italian primary, English available.

## Product Purpose
A single self-contained HTML study app, "Bersagli & Farmaci in 3D", that unifies: an interactive 3D
explorer of channels/receptors, comparison tables, drug-by-target browsing with deep links to a sister
FarmacoFlash app, mechanism animations, a CYP450/interactions reference, an antibiotic mind-map, and an
expandable "Classificazioni" library (62 exam classifications grounded in the real lecture material).
Success = the student finds and understands a target or drug class fast, trusts the content, and enjoys
using it enough to return.

## Brand Personality
Scholarly, precise, and quietly confident — a serious study instrument that feels crafted, not clinical.
Three words: rigorous, elegant, calm. It should evoke focus and trust, never noise. Bilingual and
exam-aware: every label and note reads like it came from a meticulous tutor.

## Anti-references
Generic SaaS dashboards (card-grid + gradient-metric template); "AI-slop" landing pages (purple
gradients, tiny tracked eyebrows over every section, identical icon cards); cluttered medical-software UIs;
neon "cyber" science themes. Nothing that reads as auto-generated.

## Design Principles
- Airy by default: whitespace and rhythm carry the hierarchy; the content breathes (Apple-like calm).
- Identity preserved: the gold accent and the Fraunces display voice stay; refinement, not reinvention.
- Reading is the product: contrast and type legibility win over decoration; tables and mechanisms must be
  effortless to scan.
- Depth, not chrome: soft shadows, subtle material, restrained motion — never glassmorphism-for-its-own-sake.
- Both themes first-class: light is an airy showcase, dark is a refined study mode; both meet WCAG contrast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large/UI text ≥3:1, in BOTH light and dark themes (verify placeholders
and muted text too). Full bilingual IT/EN. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (every animation needs a
reduced alternative). Keyboard-focus states visible. Do not regress the 3D explorer, FarmacoFlash links,
or any existing function.
